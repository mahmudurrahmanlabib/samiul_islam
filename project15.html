    Communication is the active relationship established between people through language, and intercultural means that this communicative relationship is between people of different cultures, where culture is the structured manifestation of human behaviour in social life within specific national and local contexts, for example: political, linguistic, economic, institutional, and professional.

    Humans have been communicating since four million years. On the other hand, the birth of culture is estimated to have taken place about 35,000 years ago (bartleby research). Today, both culture and communication have evolved considerably and have become interdependent of one another, to the point that communication is considered to be a product of culture. Thus, our own culture has a deep impact on our thoughts and behaviors. Since each culture has its distinct aspects, intercultural communication can be the cause of conflict and disorder. Generally, effective communication can be a challenge between people from different cultures and backgrounds, misunderstanding, confusion, miscommunication is among the issues that can lead to conflicts, anxiety, lack of interest etc. in intercultural communication. The biggest challenges of intercultural communication are: build rapport, communicate effectively, tolerant about people's values, intimate conversations, being aware of our bias (Quora).

    Being able to build rapport and find common grounds, so that our conversations become interesting.

    Being able to communicate effectively in a foreign language, which requires understanding slang, sarcasm, sense of humor, linguistics and jargon.

    Being tolerant about people's values and understand etiquette.

    Being able to have intimate conversations with people without arguing about topics that involve differences in values for example:  gender roles, interactions, religions etc.

    Humans have many biases that are for the most part out of awareness; but biases are not inherently negative. That's why it's so important to uncover our biases, to become aware of them so that you can determine whether or not they are reflecting something that serves you or reflecting a limiting belief or thought (JOHNSON, 2012).

    Intercultural communication is more challenging when we are working in an environment where we can find people from different cultural background. People from different cultural background have different attitudes, beliefs, values, encoding and decoding style, non-verbal cues etc. while working together, we need to keep that in our mind that we all have to communicate with one another in mutual way and don’t assume anything that we don’t understand. Rather assuming we can directly ask for clarification. Most of the time faces problem in nonverbal communication because it can mean totally a different meaning during intercultural communication.

    One can use the iceberg concept to improve their intercultural communication. Iceberg concept has three stages first one is the surface culture second is shallow culture and last one is deep culture. Surface culture is observable but shallow and deep culture is not observable. We can easily understand the surface culture because it is consisted of spoken rules but if we want to communicate in-depth, we need to understand the shallow and deep culture also because our communication will be a successful communication if we are able to interpret correctly.

    If we are thinking about cultural diversity in workplace, it is very effective for an organization. It actually decreases team conflicts and enhances creativity. If different cultural background people work together, there will be no opportunity to make similar cultural sub-groups also no scope for kinship because all of them come from different background. By this way all employee will try to deliver their best performance for the organization to maintain their position and existence. This will create a synergistic work environment so that everyone can work separately to achieve any goal. This will also support cultural competency of the staff.

    The upper part of the iceberg is the much smaller, visible part of the iceberg. In our context, this is everything that an individual shows from their culture. This is everything that we immediately recognize from the other culture that belongs exactly to that culture. Under the water level there are the much larger part of the iceberg as the invisible, not immediately recognizable part. This unknown area of culture must firstly be researched in order to prevent possible “dangers of collision” (Gemeinhardt).

    If we want to communicate effectively. This does not mean that we will not make mistakes along the way or may even completely avoid offending someone from a different culture, but we want to gain a better understanding so that when we do encounter a certain situation, we have a better understanding of how to go about it, and let the other person see that we are trying. And I believe that universally if someone from a different culture sees that you are trying your best to accommodate them and understand them that they will also likely be more open and forgive any minor mistakes. (Culture, Communication and Icebergs, 2016)

    So, to communicate effectively we need to speak effectively, try to understand other people’s perceptions and listen.
