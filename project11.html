     Share-Net Bangladesh’s 6th Knowledge Fair themed ‘Climate Change and SRHR’

     

    Dhaka,Bangladesh – 21 November 2021, To address the consequences of climate change on the sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR), Share-Net Bangladesh organized their annual event titled “Knowledge Fair on Sexual Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR)”.

    The annual flagship event started at Spectra Convention Center in Capital on Sunday.

    Addressing the event, experts from around Bangladesh gathered to discuss and express the need for more evidence and data on this burning issue.

    Climate change impacts does not only provoke more child marriage and gender-based violence, but also hampers women’s reproductive health, said climate change and health experts.

    The Managing Director of RedOrange, Arnob Chakrabarty presided over the event. Other experts including Ayesha Taasin Khan, director and head of Inclusive Citizenship and Education, Friendship; Dr Ubaidur Rob, country director of Population Council; Dr Nazneen Islam Khan, senior officer of ICCCAD; and Mushfiqua Satiar from Embassy of the Kingdom of the Netherlands (EKN) were also present at the event.

    Arnob Chakrabarty stated our studies have shown that climate change is interconnected to child marriages in marginal areas, and it is also leading to miscarriages and infertility.

    Senior officer of ICCCAD Dr Nazneen Islam Khan said, “Climate is changing much faster than before, and it causes the profound effect on our physical and mental health. Especially women who are living in the char and coastal areas.” She emphasized raising awareness on this issue from our respective positions.

    Dr Ubaidur Rob expressed the need for evidence on the issue of climate change impacts on SRHR. He also urged to initiate long and short time studies to accumulate and compilation.

    “According to research, 68% of women were more climate impacted than men because of their reproductive system in their body,” said Ayesha Taasin Khan.

    “Over time, we are encountering a lot of problems caused by salinity. We do not have such evidence, but we are noticing that there is an indirect relationship between salinity and miscarriage,” she further added.

    Experts agree that there is a link between climate change and SRHR. They need to conduct more research in this regard.

    Mushfiqua Satiar said, “As a donor, they will prioritize the proposed projects on climate change with gender equality.”

    After the two thematic sessions, young researcher fellows of Share-Net Bangladesh gave presentations on child marriage and gender-based violence.

    The event concluded with an award-giving ceremony named “SRHR Recognition of the Year 2021” award. Noorin Suhaila Asjad of Oroddho Foundation received an award in the Youth category and Muktasree Chakma Sathi of Supporting People and Rebuilding Communities (SPaRC) in the general category.

    Bangla Car: A step towards setting foot among international automobile names.

     

    Dhaka, Bangladesh – December 9, 2021, Hossain Group, one of Bangladesh’s leading conglomerates, launched the country’s first-ever “Made in Bangladesh” car, the Bangla Car.

    The Bangla Car is 7-seater SUV worth BDT 30 lakhs. This car is equipped with a 1.5 Litre Turbo Engine and a Triptonic mode power pack for shifting between manual and automatic transition. It also has a 6-speed CVT gearbox for enhanced power output and efficiency.

    It comes with 4 airbags in the steering wheel dashboard and a lower mount for extra protection. A traction control system for enabling it to take sharp turns with stability even in complex roads. A dynamic 360-degree parking camera and smart tire sensors for trouble-free parking as well as a Tire Pressure Monitoring System.

    To increase the journey experience, the Bangla car comes equipped with an Artificial Intelligent Virtual Assistant feature with more than 100 voice commands to help you reach your destination.

    This car comes with a bold sporty look packed with modern LED parking lights and is furnished with the finest leather and prime-grade materials. A 9-inch infotainment display powered by a multimedia console controller for an enhanced entertainment experience; in addition to a multi-speaker rich audio system.

    With the ambition to add a local name among renowned international car brands such as Toyota, Honda, Mitsubishi, or Nissan, Hossain Group of Industries launched their sister concern, Bangla Cars Ltd, who collaborated with companies such as DFSK Glory of Mainland China to manufacture and assemble international standard SUVs in Bangladesh.

    According to the Md. Zakir Hossain they can provide the car at a cheaper rate if the government helps them in terms of tax and if they can export the cars.

    If the manufacturing plant in established in Ponchoboti, Narayanganj, it will create almost 2000 new jobs.

    Bangla Cars Limited plans to build up an integrated base of production, marketing, and service in Bangladesh in the early stage of the project, and achieve the sales volume to be 1,500 units of new cars within 3 years by building transparent production and 24 hours after-sales-service.
